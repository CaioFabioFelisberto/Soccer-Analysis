Cold blood vs. set pieces

The chart separates open-play goals from penalties so you can judge who really carries attacking output in difficult match states.

Why it matters: a raw goal total can hide dependency on dead-ball situations.

What to trust: non-penalty production and shot quality tell a cleaner story about real striker value.

  • Target the players whose open-play contribution stays high without penalty inflation.
  • Cross-check the total goal volume with npxG to separate volume from finishing quality.
  • Use the graph to spot scorers who convert sustained pressure into real attacking output.

Insight: a striker with fewer goals can be more useful than the headline scorer if most of the volume comes from penalties.
